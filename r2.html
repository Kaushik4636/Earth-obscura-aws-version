Evolutionary Biology

The Living Museum.

The Daintree is a window into a pre-historic Earth. It is a relict of the great Gondwanan forests that once covered the supercontinent millions of years ago.

While the Amazon is celebrated for its scale, the Daintree is significant for its **ancestry**. It contains the highest concentration of primitive flowering plant families on Earth. Walking through its dense canopy is a journey back to the age of the dinosaurs, where the flora has remained largely unchanged for eons.

Primitive Lineages

The forest is home to "green dinosaurs" like the **Idiospermum australiense** (Ribbonwood), a plant that dates back 120 million years. Its existence provides critical clues into how flowering plants first evolved. The survival of these lineages is due to the region's stable climate, which sheltered the forest while the rest of the world underwent radical geological shifts.

The Reef Intersection

The Daintree is the only place on Earth where two UNESCO World Heritage sites meet—the rainforest and the Great Barrier Reef. This intersection creates a unique ecological "edge effect" where terrestrial nutrients wash directly into the marine ecosystem, supporting a highly specialized array of mangrove species that act as a biological filter between land and sea.
